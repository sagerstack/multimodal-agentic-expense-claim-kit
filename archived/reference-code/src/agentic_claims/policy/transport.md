# SUTD Transport Expense Policy

## Section 1: Approved Transport Modes

This policy governs reimbursement for business-related transportation expenses incurred by SUTD faculty, staff, and approved contractors.

### Section 1.1: Public Transport (Preferred)
Public transportation is the preferred and most cost-effective mode for local travel. Eligible public transport includes:
- Mass Rapid Transit (MRT)
- Light Rail Transit (LRT)
- Public buses
- City-operated ferries

Public transport expenses are reimbursable at **actual fare cost** with no cap, provided the journey is business-related.

### Section 1.2: Taxi and Ride-Hail Services
Taxis, Grab, Gojek, and similar ride-hail services are reimbursable when:
- Public transport is unavailable or impractical (e.g., late night, remote location, heavy equipment transport)
- Time sensitivity requires faster travel (must provide written justification)
- Traveling with external guests or clients
- Medical or accessibility needs require door-to-door transport

Ride-hail bookings must use **GrabShare or equivalent shared ride options** when traveling alone, unless justification is provided.

### Section 1.3: Private Car Mileage
Personal vehicle use for business purposes is reimbursable at the university-approved mileage rate of **SGD 0.60 per kilometer**.

Private car use requires:
- Prior approval from line manager (via Mileage Pre-Approval Form MPF-03)
- Odometer reading or GPS route verification
- Written justification for why public transport or ride-hail was not suitable

### Section 1.4: Rental Vehicles
Car rentals are reimbursable for:
- Multi-day field research or site visits
- Locations not serviced by public transport
- Transport of research equipment exceeding 20 kg

Rental vehicle bookings require department head approval and must use university-contracted vendors (see Finance portal for approved list).

### Section 1.5: Air Travel
Air travel for domestic and international business trips is covered under the separate Travel Policy (TP-2026). Short-distance air travel (under 300 km) requires VP-level approval.

## Section 2: Per-Trip Caps and Limits

### Section 2.1: MRT/Bus Actual Fare
No cap applies. Reimburse exact fare as shown on receipt or transport card transaction history.

For claims without electronic receipt (e.g., cash payment), provide route details and standard fare will be reimbursed based on published LTA fare tables.

### Section 2.2: Taxi and Ride-Hail Cap
Maximum reimbursable amount per trip: **SGD 40.00** (soft cap)

Claims exceeding SGD 40 per trip require:
- Written justification explaining why the trip exceeded the cap (e.g., traffic surcharge, airport pickup, long-distance route)
- Manager approval via Expense Override Form (EOF-04)

Claims above **SGD 80 per trip** require department head approval and are subject to audit review.

### Section 2.3: Peak Hour Surcharges
Peak hour and midnight surcharges for taxis and ride-hail services are reimbursable. Electronic receipt must show the surcharge breakdown.

### Section 2.4: ERP and Toll Charges
Electronic Road Pricing (ERP) charges, toll fees, and parking fees are reimbursable when incurred during business travel. Submit itemized receipt or ERP transaction statement.

### Section 2.5: Private Car Mileage Rate
Reimbursement rate: **SGD 0.60 per kilometer**

This rate includes:
- Fuel costs
- Vehicle wear and tear
- Insurance and maintenance (averaged)

Additional claims for fuel, parking, or tolls are NOT permitted when claiming mileage reimbursement. The SGD 0.60/km rate is all-inclusive.

### Section 2.6: Parking
Parking fees at business destinations are reimbursable at actual cost. Overnight parking requires written justification. Monthly parking pass costs are NOT reimbursable.

## Section 3: Airport Transfers

### Section 3.1: Economy Class Only
Airport transfers must use economy or standard service options:
- MRT (preferred, actual fare)
- Public bus (preferred, actual fare)
- Taxi or Grab (standard metered fare, up to SGD 40 cap)

Limousine services, premium ride-hail options (e.g., GrabPremium, Grab6), and private car services require VP approval and are subject to strict audit.

### Section 3.2: Airport Lounge Access
Airport lounge access fees are NOT reimbursable. Use of personal credit card lounge benefits is permitted but not claimable.

### Section 3.3: Baggage and Trolley Fees
Baggage trolley fees and porter services are NOT reimbursable. Check university travel luggage guidelines for carry-on recommendations.

## Section 4: Overseas Transport

For international business travel, local transport expenses are reimbursable under the following conditions:

### Section 4.1: Public Transport Preferred
Always use public transport (metro, bus, train) when available at the destination. Actual fare is reimbursable with receipt or transport card statement.

### Section 4.2: Hotel Shuttle Services
Hotel-provided shuttle services to/from airport or conference venue are preferred and fully reimbursable. Submit shuttle booking confirmation or hotel invoice showing the charge.

### Section 4.3: Taxi and Ride-Hail (Overseas)
Overseas taxi and ride-hail expenses are reimbursable at actual cost, subject to:
- Written justification for trips exceeding local equivalent of SGD 40
- Receipt in original currency (auto-converted to SGD at submission date exchange rate)
- Destination address and business purpose noted on claim form

### Section 4.4: Rental Cars (Overseas)
Overseas rental cars require:
- Department head pre-approval
- Insurance coverage through university-approved provider or credit card coverage proof
- Fuel receipts (if not included in rental rate)

Rental car reimbursement includes vehicle rental, mandatory insurance, and fuel. Optional add-ons (GPS, child seats, premium insurance) are NOT reimbursable.

### Section 4.5: Train and Intercity Rail
High-speed rail and intercity train travel is reimbursable at **economy or second-class fare**. First-class or business-class rail tickets require department head approval and written justification.

## Section 5: Commute Exclusion

### Section 5.1: Daily Home-to-Office Commute
Regular daily commute between home and primary workplace is NOT reimbursable. This includes:
- Morning travel from home to SUTD campus
- Evening travel from SUTD campus to home
- Lunch-hour personal errands

Commute exclusion applies even if working overtime or attending evening events at the office.

### Section 5.2: Travel to Secondary Work Sites
Travel from home to a secondary work site (not primary workplace) IS reimbursable:
- Field research locations
- Off-campus meeting venues
- Client or partner offices
- Conference or training venues

Submit location details and business purpose with the claim.

### Section 5.3: Multi-Leg Journey Calculation
For journeys with multiple stops (e.g., home → client office → SUTD campus → home):
- Exclude the home-to-office leg (commute)
- Reimburse only the business travel portion (office → client office)

Use route maps or GPS logs to support multi-leg journey claims.

## Section 6: Required Documentation

All transport expense claims must include:

### Section 6.1: Electronic Receipt
For ride-hail services (Grab, Gojek, Uber):
- Screenshot or PDF of electronic receipt showing:
  - Date and time of trip
  - Pickup and drop-off addresses
  - Itemized fare breakdown (base fare, surcharges, tolls)
  - Payment method

### Section 6.2: Taxi Receipt
For metered taxis:
- Physical or photographed receipt showing:
  - Taxi license plate number
  - Date and time
  - Total fare (including surcharges and ERP)

Credit card statements are NOT acceptable as primary receipt.

### Section 6.3: Public Transport Proof
For MRT/bus claims:
- SimplyGo or transit card transaction history (download from TransitLink app or website)
- Entry and exit stations with timestamp
- Fare amount

For cash payments, provide route details (origin and destination stations) and standard fare will be reimbursed per LTA published rates.

### Section 6.4: Mileage Log
For private car mileage claims:
- Completed Mileage Log Form (MLF-05) showing:
  - Date of travel
  - Start and end odometer readings
  - Origin and destination addresses
  - Total kilometers traveled
  - Business purpose

GPS route proof (e.g., Google Maps screenshot with distance) may be submitted in lieu of odometer readings if approved by manager.

### Section 6.5: Route and Purpose
All transport claims must include:
- Brief description of business purpose (1-2 sentences)
- Destination address or venue name
- Justification for mode choice (if not public transport)

Claims without clear business purpose will be rejected.

### Section 6.6: Group Travel
For shared taxi/ride-hail with multiple SUTD employees:
- One employee submits the full receipt
- Receipt copy shared with other travelers (marked "copy - reimbursed by [Employee ID]")
- Each traveler's claim notes the cost-sharing arrangement

Duplicate reimbursement of the same trip is flagged as fraud (see General Expense Policy Section 4).
