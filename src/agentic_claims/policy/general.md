# SUTD General Expense Policy

## Section 1: Submission Deadline

All expense claims must be submitted within **30 calendar days** from the date of expense incurrence.

### Section 1.1: 30-Day Hard Deadline
Expenses older than 30 days are NOT reimbursable. No exceptions will be granted, regardless of:
- Claim amount
- Employee seniority
- Business justification
- External circumstances

Plan submission deadlines accordingly, especially for month-end expenses or during leave periods.

### Section 1.2: Submission Date Calculation
The 30-day period begins on:
- For single-day expenses: the date on the receipt
- For multi-day trips: the return date (last day of travel)
- For recurring subscriptions: the invoice date

Example: A 5-day conference from March 10-14, 2026 must have all related expenses (accommodation, transport, meals) submitted by April 13, 2026.

### Section 1.3: Submission During Leave
If you are on approved leave (vacation, medical, parental) during the 30-day window:
- Submit expenses before leave begins, OR
- Delegate submission authority to your line manager (complete Expense Delegation Form EDF-09), OR
- Submit immediately upon return from leave

Leave is NOT an acceptable reason for late submission if the 30-day deadline expires during leave.

### Section 1.4: Batch Submission Encouraged
Employees are encouraged to batch-submit expenses weekly or bi-weekly to:
- Avoid last-minute rush before the 30-day deadline
- Reduce administrative burden
- Improve cash flow planning

Multiple receipts from the same trip or project can be submitted as a single claim batch.

### Section 1.5: Submission Channels
Expense claims must be submitted via:
- SUTD Expense Portal (online submission, preferred)
- Manual paper claim form (for employees without portal access)

Email submissions of scanned receipts are NOT accepted unless portal is experiencing system downtime (requires Finance approval).

## Section 2: Currency Handling

All expense claims must be submitted in **Singapore Dollars (SGD)**. Foreign currency expenses will be automatically converted at submission.

### Section 2.1: Auto-Conversion on Submission Date
Foreign currency receipts are converted to SGD using the exchange rate on the **date of submission** (not the date of expense).

The university expense system uses Frankfurter.app exchange rates (European Central Bank reference rates) for conversion.

### Section 2.2: Exchange Rate Display
When submitting a foreign currency expense, the system will display:
- Original amount and currency (e.g., USD 150.00)
- Exchange rate applied (e.g., 1 USD = 1.35 SGD)
- Converted SGD amount (e.g., SGD 202.50)
- Conversion date (submission date)

Review the converted amount before finalizing submission.

### Section 2.3: Manual Conversion (Paper Claims)
For manual paper claims with foreign currency receipts:
- Use the SUTD Currency Converter tool (available on Finance portal)
- Enter original amount, currency, and submission date
- Print the conversion summary and attach to claim form

Do NOT use personal currency conversion tools (Google, xe.com, bank rates) for official claims. Only university-approved converter rates are accepted.

### Section 2.4: Multi-Currency Claims
If a single trip involves expenses in multiple currencies (e.g., EUR hotel, GBP meals, USD transport), submit each currency separately:
- System will convert each expense individually
- Final claim total in SGD is the sum of all converted amounts

### Section 2.5: Dynamic vs. Static Rates
Exchange rates fluctuate daily. The rate applied on submission date may differ from the rate on expense date. This is expected and accepted.

Employees bear the currency risk for delayed submission. Submit promptly to minimize exchange rate volatility impact.

## Section 3: Approval Thresholds

Expense claims are subject to tiered approval based on total claim amount.

### Section 3.1: Auto-Approval (Under SGD 200)
Claims under **SGD 200** are automatically approved if:
- All policy requirements are met (caps, documentation, business purpose)
- No red flags detected (duplicate claim, suspicious pattern)
- Submission is within 30-day deadline

Auto-approved claims are processed within 3-5 business days.

### Section 3.2: Line Manager Approval (SGD 200 - SGD 1,000)
Claims between **SGD 200 and SGD 1,000** require line manager approval.

Line manager reviews:
- Business justification and purpose
- Policy compliance (meal caps, transport modes, accommodation rates)
- Receipt authenticity and completeness

Manager must approve or reject within **5 business days**. Delayed manager approval does NOT extend the 30-day submission deadline.

### Section 3.3: Department Head Approval (Over SGD 1,000)
Claims exceeding **SGD 1,000** require department head approval.

Department head reviews:
- Strategic alignment with departmental budget
- Cost-effectiveness and business necessity
- High-value expense justification (e.g., international conference, multi-day research trip)

Department head must approve or reject within **7 business days**.

### Section 3.4: Exception Approvals
Expenses that violate policy caps (meal over SGD 30, taxi over SGD 40, hotel over tier cap) require **exception approval** via Expense Override Form (EOF-04), regardless of total claim amount.

Exception approvals are routed to department head or VP depending on policy violation severity.

### Section 3.5: Split Claims to Avoid Approval Tiers
Intentionally splitting a single trip or project into multiple smaller claims to avoid higher approval tiers is prohibited. Finance audits detect split claim patterns.

Example of prohibited behavior:
- SGD 800 conference trip split into 3 claims (SGD 300, SGD 250, SGD 250) to avoid SGD 1,000 threshold

Submit all related expenses from a single trip/project as one claim.

## Section 4: Duplicate Claims and Fraud Detection

### Section 4.1: Zero Tolerance for Duplicate Claims
Submitting the same expense more than once (intentionally or accidentally) is considered fraud.

Duplicate claim triggers:
- Same receipt amount, date, and vendor submitted twice
- Group travel where multiple employees claim the same shared expense without annotation (see group meal/transport policies)

First-time duplicate claims may be flagged as accidental error (claim rejected, no penalty). Repeated duplicate claims result in disciplinary action.

### Section 4.2: Fraud Detection System
The expense system uses automated fraud detection to flag:
- Duplicate receipts (amount, date, merchant matching)
- Round-number claims (suspiciously frequent SGD X.00 amounts with no cents)
- Rapid-fire submissions (10+ claims submitted within 1 hour)
- Policy cap violations (claims systematically at or near caps)
- Unusual merchant names (non-existent vendors, suspicious patterns)

Flagged claims are routed to Finance Compliance team for manual review.

### Section 4.3: Receipt Forgery
Submitting forged, altered, or fabricated receipts is fraud and grounds for termination.

Compliance audits include:
- Random vendor verification (Finance calls merchants to confirm transactions)
- Receipt format analysis (detecting photoshopped or generated receipts)
- Cross-referencing credit card statements

Employees suspected of receipt forgery are subject to full audit and disciplinary process.

### Section 4.4: Whistleblower Protection
Employees who witness or suspect expense fraud are encouraged to report anonymously via:
- SUTD Ethics Hotline (ethics@sutd.edu.sg)
- Anonymous web form (sutd.edu.sg/report-concern)

Whistleblower identity is protected. Retaliation against whistleblowers is prohibited and subject to disciplinary action.

## Section 5: Audit and Compliance

### Section 5.1: Random Audit Program
Finance conducts random audits of **10% of all approved claims each quarter**.

Random audit process:
- Finance selects claims using random sampling algorithm (stratified by department and amount tier)
- Auditors verify:
  - Receipt authenticity (vendor confirmation)
  - Business purpose legitimacy (event attendance proof, meeting notes)
  - Policy compliance (caps, documentation, GL codes)
- Audit findings report sent to department head and employee

### Section 5.2: Targeted Audits
In addition to random audits, Finance may conduct targeted audits based on:
- Fraud detection flags (Section 4.2)
- Unusually high claim volume or amounts from specific employee
- Department-wide spending pattern anomalies
- External audit recommendations

Employees selected for targeted audit will be notified and required to provide additional documentation.

### Section 5.3: Audit Findings and Consequences
Audit outcomes:
- **Compliant**: No action required
- **Minor violation**: Warning issued, employee required to attend policy training
- **Major violation**: Claim reversal (reimbursement clawed back), written warning, potential suspension
- **Fraud**: Termination, legal action, reporting to authorities

### Section 5.4: Record Retention for Audit
All expense claims and supporting documentation must be retained for **7 years** in accordance with university financial record-keeping policy.

Employees are responsible for:
- Retaining digital or physical copies of receipts for 7 years
- Providing documents if requested during audit (even for claims from prior years)

Failure to produce records during audit is considered non-compliance.

### Section 5.5: Quarterly Compliance Reports
Finance publishes quarterly expense compliance reports showing:
- Total expense volume by department
- Average claim processing time
- Rejection rate and top rejection reasons
- Audit findings summary (anonymized)

Departments with high rejection or violation rates are flagged for policy training and process improvement.

## Section 6: Appeals Process

Employees may appeal rejected claims within **14 calendar days** of rejection notification.

### Section 6.1: Appeal Submission
To appeal a rejected claim:
- Submit Appeal Request Form (ARF-10) via expense portal
- Provide:
  - Original claim reference number
  - Detailed explanation of why the rejection should be overturned
  - Additional supporting evidence (e.g., corrected receipt, manager approval, policy interpretation justification)

### Section 6.2: Appeal Review Process
Appeals are reviewed by:
- Finance Compliance Manager (for policy interpretation disputes)
- Department Head (for business justification disputes)
- VP Finance (for high-value or complex appeals)

Appeal review timeline: **10 business days** from appeal submission.

### Section 6.3: Appeal Outcomes
Possible appeal outcomes:
- **Approved**: Claim reinstated and processed for payment
- **Partially Approved**: Claim approved for reduced amount (e.g., reimbursed up to policy cap instead of full amount)
- **Denied**: Rejection upheld, no reimbursement

Appeal decisions are final. No further appeals are permitted.

### Section 6.4: Common Appeal Scenarios
Appeals are most commonly successful for:
- Lost receipt claims with strong supporting evidence (bank statement, vendor confirmation)
- Policy interpretation ambiguity (e.g., borderline meal cap, unclear transport mode eligibility)
- Extenuating circumstances with documented proof (medical emergency, natural disaster, university-mandated last-minute trip)

Appeals are rarely successful for:
- Late submission (30-day deadline is non-negotiable)
- Deliberate policy violations (e.g., booking luxury hotel knowing it exceeds cap)
- Insufficient documentation (employee's responsibility to retain receipts)

### Section 6.5: Appeal Statistics
Finance tracks appeal metrics:
- Appeal volume by department and claim type
- Appeal approval rate
- Common appeal reasons

Departments with high appeal volume are flagged for additional policy training.
