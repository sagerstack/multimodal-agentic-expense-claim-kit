# SUTD Meal Expense Policy

## Section 1: Scope and Eligibility

This policy applies to all SUTD faculty, staff, and approved contractors who incur meal expenses during university-related business activities. Eligible activities include:

- Official meetings with external partners, vendors, or collaborators
- Conference attendance (domestic or international)
- Field research activities requiring overnight stay
- Project-related travel approved by department head
- Business entertainment of prospective students, donors, or industry partners

Meal claims for personal purposes, social gatherings, or routine daily meals at the workplace are NOT eligible.

## Section 2: Daily Meal Caps

All meal reimbursements are subject to the following daily caps per meal type:

### Section 2.1: Breakfast
Maximum reimbursable amount: **SGD 15.00** per person (hard cap)

Breakfast claims must be accompanied by a receipt showing date, time, and itemized list if total exceeds SGD 10.

### Section 2.2: Lunch
Maximum reimbursable amount: **SGD 20.00** per person (hard cap)

Lunch claims require original receipt with merchant name and date. For claims above SGD 15, itemized breakdown is mandatory.

### Section 2.3: Dinner
Maximum reimbursable amount: **SGD 30.00** per person (hard cap)

Dinner claims must include original receipt with full itemization if total exceeds SGD 20. Claims for dinner before 6:00 PM require written justification.

### Section 2.4: Total Daily Cap
Maximum total meal reimbursement per day: **SGD 50.00** per person

This cap applies to the sum of breakfast, lunch, and dinner claims on a single calendar day. Claims exceeding SGD 50 in aggregate will be rejected unless pre-approved by department head for exceptional circumstances.

## Section 3: Business Meal Entertainment

Business meals involving external guests or clients are subject to additional rules:

### Section 3.1: Pre-Approval Requirement
Meals exceeding **SGD 100 total** (for all attendees combined) require advance approval via the Expense Pre-Approval Form (EPF-01). Submit at least 3 business days prior to the event.

### Section 3.2: Attendee Limit
Maximum **8 attendees** per business meal. Meals with more than 8 attendees require written justification and VP-level approval.

### Section 3.3: Documentation
All business meal claims must include:
- Original itemized receipt showing food/beverage breakdown
- List of attendees with names and affiliations
- Brief description of business purpose (1-2 sentences)
- For claims above SGD 200: meeting agenda or formal invitation

### Section 3.4: Gratuity and Service Charges
Service charges and gratuity are reimbursable up to 15% of the pre-tax meal cost. Tips exceeding 15% require written justification.

## Section 4: Overseas Meal Allowances

For approved international travel, meal caps are adjusted as follows:

### Section 4.1: Approved Destinations
Overseas meal allowance applies to destinations listed in the University Travel Destination Register. For unlisted destinations, contact Finance for per diem rates.

### Section 4.2: Overseas Multiplier
Meal caps for overseas travel are **1.5x domestic caps**:
- Breakfast: SGD 22.50
- Lunch: SGD 30.00
- Dinner: SGD 45.00
- Total daily cap: SGD 75.00

### Section 4.3: Currency Conversion
All foreign currency receipts will be converted to SGD at the exchange rate on the date of expense. Use the university's automated currency converter or submit exchange rate proof from xe.com or OANDA.

### Section 4.4: Meals Included in Conference Registration
If conference registration includes meals, those meals are NOT claimable separately. Conference agenda must be attached to verify included meal schedule.

## Section 5: Prohibited Items

The following items are NOT reimbursable under the meal expense policy:

### Section 5.1: Alcohol on Personal Claims
Alcoholic beverages are NOT reimbursable for individual meals. Alcohol may be approved for business entertainment meals (Section 3) with advance approval and written justification.

### Section 5.2: Tobacco Products
Cigarettes, cigars, vaping products, and all tobacco-related items are strictly non-reimbursable.

### Section 5.3: Personal Groceries
Groceries, meal kits, or food items purchased for home consumption are not eligible, even during business travel. Only restaurant or prepared meal receipts qualify.

### Section 5.4: Minibar and In-Room Dining
Hotel minibar charges and in-room dining are NOT reimbursable unless pre-approved for exceptional circumstances (e.g., mandatory quarantine, medical restriction).

### Section 5.5: Non-Meal Items
Purchases of non-food items on the same receipt (e.g., toiletries, magazines, gifts) must be excluded from the claimed amount. Submit itemized receipt with non-meal items clearly marked as excluded.

## Section 6: Required Documentation

All meal expense claims must be submitted with the following documentation:

### Section 6.1: Original Receipt
Original receipt (physical or electronic) showing:
- Merchant name and location
- Date and time of purchase
- Itemized list of food and beverages (required for claims above SGD 30)
- Payment method and total amount

Credit card statements are NOT acceptable as primary documentation.

### Section 6.2: Receipt Retention
Retain original receipts for 7 years in accordance with university financial record-keeping policy. Digital scans or photos must be clear and legible.

### Section 6.3: Lost Receipt Affidavit
For lost receipts, submit Lost Receipt Affidavit (LRA-02) with the following:
- Written explanation of loss circumstances
- Estimated meal cost and date
- Credit card or bank statement showing the transaction
- Manager approval

Lost receipt claims are limited to **2 per fiscal year** per employee.

### Section 6.4: Group Meal Documentation
For group meals (2 or more SUTD employees), submit:
- Single receipt with total amount
- List of all attendees with employee IDs
- Reimbursement split among attendees (per-person breakdown)

Each attendee's individual claim must not exceed their daily cap (Section 2.4).
