# SUTD Office Supplies Expense Policy

## Section 1: Approved Categories

This policy covers reimbursement for office supplies and small-value equipment purchases made by SUTD faculty and staff for business purposes.

### Section 1.1: Stationery
Reimbursable stationery items include:
- Pens, pencils, markers, highlighters
- Notebooks, notepads, sticky notes
- Folders, binders, filing supplies
- Paper (printer paper, specialty paper)
- Envelopes, mailing supplies
- Desk organizers, paper clips, staplers

Stationery purchases must be for university business use. Personal stationery is NOT reimbursable.

### Section 1.2: Printing and Copying
Reimbursable printing expenses include:
- External print shop services (posters, banners, conference materials)
- Business cards (university-branded, department-approved design)
- Binding and laminating services
- Color printing for presentations or educational materials

Personal printing (family photos, personal documents) is NOT reimbursable.

### Section 1.3: Software Licenses (Under SGD 50)
Small-value software subscriptions and licenses are reimbursable if:
- Annual cost is **under SGD 50**
- Software is required for specific business task (not general-purpose)
- IT department does not provide a university license for the tool

Examples: stock photo subscriptions, grammar checkers (Grammarly), PDF editors, domain name registration.

Software over SGD 50 must go through IT procurement process.

### Section 1.4: Other Office Items
Reimbursable miscellaneous office supplies:
- Batteries (for office equipment, presentation remotes)
- USB drives, external hard drives (under SGD 50, see Section 2.1)
- Cables and adapters (HDMI, USB-C, etc.)
- Whiteboards, markers, erasers
- Name tags, lanyards for events

### Section 1.5: Non-Approved Categories
The following are NOT covered under this policy:
- Furniture (desks, chairs, shelves) – use Facilities procurement
- Major technology equipment (laptops, monitors, printers) – use IT procurement
- Catering and food supplies – use Meal Expense Policy
- Event supplies (decorations, gifts) – use Event Budget process
- Books and journals – use Library or Research Budget

## Section 2: Per-Item Cap and Bulk Purchases

### Section 2.1: Per-Item Cap
Maximum reimbursable amount per single item: **SGD 100**

Items priced above SGD 100 (before taxes) must be purchased through the university procurement process, not via personal reimbursement.

Examples:
- USB drive priced at SGD 35 – reimbursable
- External monitor priced at SGD 250 – NOT reimbursable via expense claim (use IT procurement)
- Business card printing (500 cards) for SGD 80 – reimbursable

### Section 2.2: Bulk Purchase Threshold
Total purchases exceeding **SGD 500 in a single transaction** require:
- **3 vendor quotes** (price comparison from 3 different suppliers)
- Written justification for quantity and business need
- Department head approval via Bulk Purchase Form (BPF-07)

Bulk purchases are defined as:
- Multiple units of the same item (e.g., 50 notebooks)
- Large quantity of mixed supplies (e.g., office starter kit for new lab)

### Section 2.3: Single-Receipt Rule
If a single receipt contains multiple items and total exceeds SGD 500, the bulk purchase rule applies. Submit the 3 quotes and approval form even if each individual item is under the per-item cap.

### Section 2.4: Split Purchases
Intentionally splitting a bulk purchase across multiple transactions or dates to avoid the SGD 500 threshold is prohibited and considered policy violation. Finance conducts quarterly audits to detect split purchase patterns.

## Section 3: Technology Equipment Exclusions

### Section 3.1: Laptops and Computers
Laptops, desktop computers, tablets, and all-in-one workstations are NOT reimbursable under office supplies policy. Use IT procurement process:
- Submit IT Request Form (ITR-08) to SUTD IT department
- IT evaluates business need and provides university-standard equipment
- University-owned equipment includes warranty, support, and asset tracking

Personal laptop purchases will NOT be reimbursed even if used for work.

### Section 3.2: Monitors and Displays
External monitors, projectors, and display screens are NOT reimbursable. Request through IT procurement.

Exception: Small presentation accessories (wireless presentation remotes, HDMI adapters) under SGD 50 are reimbursable under Section 1.4.

### Section 3.3: Printers and Scanners
Printers, scanners, and multifunction devices are NOT reimbursable. Contact IT for department-shared printing solutions or use campus print centers.

Small portable document scanners under SGD 100 may be approved on case-by-case basis with written justification (e.g., field research requiring on-site scanning).

### Section 3.4: Networking and Storage
The following items require IT procurement, not expense reimbursement:
- Network switches, routers, Wi-Fi access points
- Network-attached storage (NAS) devices
- Server hardware
- Backup drives over 1TB

USB drives and portable SSDs under SGD 100 are reimbursable (Section 1.4).

### Section 3.5: Software Over SGD 50
Software, subscriptions, and cloud services exceeding SGD 50 annually must be procured through IT department:
- IT evaluates software license terms and security compliance
- Negotiates volume discounts for university-wide tools
- Ensures software is compatible with university systems

Request software via IT Service Portal.

## Section 4: Consumables and Recurring Purchases

### Section 4.1: Printer Ink and Toner
Printer ink cartridges and toner are NOT reimbursable for personal printers. Use campus print centers or request IT-managed printer installation.

Ink/toner for approved departmental printers should be purchased via IT procurement or department budget (not personal reimbursement).

### Section 4.2: Recurring Monthly Subscriptions
Recurring monthly subscriptions (e.g., cloud storage, productivity tools) under SGD 5/month are reimbursable for up to **6 months**. After 6 months, convert to IT procurement for annual billing and budget allocation.

One-time annual subscriptions under SGD 50 are reimbursable immediately (no 6-month waiting period).

### Section 4.3: Specialty Materials
Research-specific consumables (lab supplies, prototyping materials, specialized paper) should be claimed under research project budgets, not office supplies policy.

Consult with Finance or Research Office to determine correct budget code.

## Section 5: General Ledger Codes by Category

All office supply claims must include the appropriate GL code on the expense claim form.

### Section 5.1: Stationery
GL Code: **GL-4100 (Office Stationery)**

Use for:
- Writing instruments, paper, folders
- Desk organizers and general stationery

### Section 5.2: Printing Services
GL Code: **GL-4200 (Printing and Reproduction)**

Use for:
- External print shop services
- Business cards, posters, banners
- Binding, laminating, photocopying

### Section 5.3: Software and Subscriptions
GL Code: **GL-4300 (Software and Digital Tools)**

Use for:
- Software licenses and subscriptions under SGD 50
- Domain name registration
- Online tool subscriptions

### Section 5.4: Miscellaneous Office Supplies
GL Code: **GL-4100 (Office Stationery)**

Use for:
- Batteries, USB drives, cables
- Whiteboards and markers
- Any office items not covered by GL-4200 or GL-4300

If unsure which GL code to use, contact Finance for guidance. Incorrect GL codes delay reimbursement processing.

## Section 6: Required Documentation

All office supply claims must include:

### Section 6.1: Original Receipt
Original purchase receipt (physical or electronic) showing:
- Vendor name and location
- Date of purchase
- Itemized list of products with individual prices
- Total amount (including taxes)
- Payment method

Credit card statements are NOT acceptable as primary documentation.

### Section 6.2: Item Description and Quantity
The receipt must show:
- Product name or SKU
- Quantity purchased
- Unit price

For bulk purchases, highlight the total quantity to demonstrate the SGD 500 threshold calculation.

### Section 6.3: Business Justification
Provide a brief description of the business purpose:
- "Stationery for student workshop on March 15"
- "Business cards for ICSE 2026 conference"
- "HDMI cable for seminar room presentation setup"

Generic justifications ("office use") are NOT sufficient. Specify the project, event, or task.

### Section 6.4: Bulk Purchase Documentation
For purchases over SGD 500, attach:
- **3 vendor quotes** (price comparison from different suppliers)
- Completed Bulk Purchase Form (BPF-07) with department head signature
- Written justification explaining quantity and business need

Missing bulk purchase documentation will result in claim rejection.

### Section 6.5: Software License Proof
For software purchases, attach:
- License agreement or subscription confirmation email
- Invoice showing license duration (monthly, annual)
- Screenshot of software in use (if requested by Finance for audit)

### Section 6.6: GL Code Annotation
Clearly indicate the GL code on the expense claim form (see Section 5). If the receipt contains mixed categories (e.g., stationery + printing), annotate which items map to which GL code:
- GL-4100: Pens, notebooks (SGD 25)
- GL-4200: Poster printing (SGD 60)
