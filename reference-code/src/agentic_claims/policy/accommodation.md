# SUTD Accommodation Expense Policy

## Section 1: Eligibility

Accommodation expenses are reimbursable when overnight stay is required for university business purposes.

### Section 1.1: Distance Criterion
Overnight accommodation is eligible when the business destination is:
- More than **80 kilometers** from the employee's primary workplace (SUTD campus), OR
- More than **80 kilometers** from the employee's registered home address

For destinations within 80 km, overnight stay requires department head approval with written justification (e.g., early morning meeting, late-night event).

### Section 1.2: Late-Night Event Criterion
Accommodation is eligible when a business event (meeting, conference, workshop) ends after **10:00 PM** and returning home is impractical or unsafe.

Late-night event accommodation requires:
- Event agenda or invitation showing end time
- Written justification explaining why immediate return was not feasible

### Section 1.3: Multi-Day Events
Conferences, training programs, or field research spanning multiple days automatically qualify for accommodation reimbursement for the duration of the event, plus one night before (if travel day) and one night after (if return travel day).

### Section 1.4: Non-Eligible Scenarios
Accommodation is NOT reimbursable for:
- Personal leisure or vacation extensions
- Upgrade to higher room category for personal preference
- Accommodation for family members or guests (unless pre-approved as part of official hosting duties)

## Section 2: Nightly Rate Caps by Tier

All accommodation reimbursements are subject to nightly rate caps based on destination tier.

### Section 2.1: Singapore (Domestic)
Maximum nightly rate: **SGD 250 per night** (excluding taxes)

Singapore tier includes:
- All hotels and serviced apartments within Singapore
- Government service tax (GST) is reimbursable on top of the SGD 250 cap

Claims exceeding SGD 250/night require written justification and department head approval. Approved reasons include:
- Conference venue hotel sold out; nearest alternative is more expensive
- University-contracted hotel rate exceeded due to peak season
- Medical or accessibility requirements necessitate specific property

### Section 2.2: Southeast Asia
Maximum nightly rate: **SGD 200 per night** (excluding taxes)

Southeast Asia tier includes:
- Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines, Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, Brunei

Local taxes (VAT, hotel tax, city tax) are reimbursable on top of the SGD 200 cap. Submit itemized hotel invoice showing tax breakdown.

### Section 2.3: Other International Destinations
Maximum nightly rate: **SGD 350 per night** (excluding taxes)

Other international tier includes all destinations outside Singapore and Southeast Asia.

Higher cost cities (e.g., London, New York, Tokyo, Zurich) may require rate exceptions. Submit cost benchmarking (e.g., 3 hotel quotes) with the pre-approval request.

### Section 2.4: Rate Calculation
The nightly rate is the **room rate only**, excluding:
- Taxes (reimbursable separately)
- Breakfast (if included, not separately claimable under meal policy)
- Resort fees, facility fees, or mandatory service charges (reimbursable on top of cap)

### Section 2.5: Currency Conversion
Foreign currency hotel invoices will be converted to SGD at the exchange rate on the check-out date. Use university currency converter or submit exchange rate proof.

## Section 3: Booking Requirements

### Section 3.1: University-Contracted Hotels (Preferred)
Always check the SUTD Preferred Hotel Directory (available on Finance portal) before booking. University-contracted hotels offer:
- Pre-negotiated rates (often below policy caps)
- Direct billing to university (no upfront payment required)
- Guaranteed availability during peak conference periods

Using non-contracted hotels requires written justification (e.g., contracted hotel fully booked, event venue requires on-site accommodation).

### Section 3.2: Airbnb and Alternative Accommodation
Airbnb, serviced apartments, and vacation rentals are reimbursable with **prior approval** via Alternative Accommodation Request Form (AAR-06).

Approval criteria:
- Cost is significantly lower than hotel options (submit price comparison)
- Extended stay (5+ nights) where apartment is more practical
- Group accommodation for team research trips (requires list of occupants)

Airbnb bookings must:
- Include host name, property address, and booking confirmation
- Provide itemized invoice showing nightly rate and cleaning fees
- Exclude personal leisure bookings (strictly business purpose)

### Section 3.3: Booking Timing
Book accommodation at least **7 days in advance** to qualify for early-bird rates. Last-minute bookings (within 48 hours) that exceed policy caps will be reimbursed only up to the cap unless justified by emergency circumstances.

### Section 3.4: Cancellation Policies
Choose flexible or free cancellation rates when possible. Non-refundable bookings are reimbursable only if:
- Trip cancellation was due to university decision (project cancelled, event postponed)
- Medical emergency (doctor's note required)
- Travel restrictions (government advisory, visa denial)

Personal cancellations (change of plans, personal schedule conflict) are NOT reimbursable.

## Section 4: Incidentals

### Section 4.1: Laundry Services
Hotel laundry and dry cleaning are reimbursable for trips exceeding **5 consecutive nights**. Maximum reimbursable laundry cost: **SGD 30 per trip**.

Submit itemized laundry invoice. Personal laundry for trips under 5 nights is NOT reimbursable.

### Section 4.2: Minibar
Minibar charges are NOT reimbursable unless pre-approved for exceptional circumstances (e.g., mandatory quarantine with meal delivery unavailable, medical dietary restriction requiring specific items).

### Section 4.3: Room Service
Room service meals are NOT reimbursable. Use the meal expense policy (Meals Policy Section 1) and dine at hotel restaurant or external venues. Exception: mandatory quarantine or medical restriction (requires doctor's note).

### Section 4.4: Internet and Wi-Fi
Hotel Wi-Fi charges are reimbursable if not included in the room rate. Maximum: **SGD 15 per night**.

If hotel offers free Wi-Fi, paid premium/high-speed upgrades are NOT reimbursable.

### Section 4.5: Parking
Hotel parking fees are reimbursable for business travel requiring personal vehicle use (pre-approved via Mileage Form). Valet parking is reimbursable only if self-parking is unavailable.

### Section 4.6: Fitness Center, Spa, and Entertainment
The following hotel amenities are NOT reimbursable:
- Gym or fitness center fees (if not included)
- Spa treatments, massages, salon services
- Pay-per-view movies, in-room entertainment
- Gift shop purchases

### Section 4.7: Phone Calls
International or long-distance phone calls for business purposes are reimbursable. Submit call log with:
- Date, time, duration of call
- Number called and business purpose
- Itemized hotel phone bill

Personal calls (to family, friends) are NOT reimbursable. Use personal mobile phone or communication apps (WhatsApp, Skype) for personal calls.

## Section 5: Extended Stay

### Section 5.1: Extended Stay Threshold
Accommodation stays exceeding **5 consecutive nights** require department head approval before booking.

Extended stays must include:
- Justification for duration (e.g., week-long conference, field research schedule, training program)
- Cost comparison showing extended-stay rates vs. nightly rates
- Expected cost savings or business necessity

### Section 5.2: Weekly or Monthly Rates
For stays exceeding 7 nights, negotiate weekly or monthly rates with the hotel. Many hotels offer discounted rates for extended stays. Submit rate negotiation correspondence with the claim.

### Section 5.3: Serviced Apartments for Extended Stay
For stays exceeding 14 nights, consider serviced apartments or corporate housing. Finance can assist with vendor recommendations. Serviced apartment rates must still comply with nightly rate caps (prorated from monthly rate).

## Section 6: Required Documentation

All accommodation claims must include:

### Section 6.1: Hotel Invoice
Original hotel invoice (folio) showing:
- Hotel name, address, and contact information
- Check-in and check-out dates (with timestamps if available)
- Nightly room rate (itemized per night if multi-night stay)
- Taxes and fees breakdown (GST, city tax, resort fee, etc.)
- Total amount charged
- Payment method (credit card or direct billing confirmation)

Credit card statements are NOT acceptable as primary documentation.

### Section 6.2: Itemized Charges
If the invoice includes incidentals (meals, laundry, phone calls, parking), ensure the invoice provides an itemized breakdown. Highlight or annotate which charges are business-related vs. personal.

Personal charges must be excluded from the reimbursement amount. If unable to separate charges, request an amended invoice from the hotel.

### Section 6.3: Booking Confirmation
Attach booking confirmation email or voucher showing:
- Booking date (to verify advance booking compliance)
- Room type and rate
- Cancellation policy

### Section 6.4: Business Purpose
Provide a brief description (1-2 sentences) of the business purpose for the trip:
- Conference name and dates
- Client or partner meeting details
- Field research project name
- Training program title

### Section 6.5: Airbnb Documentation
For Airbnb or alternative accommodation, include:
- Booking confirmation with property address
- Host name and contact information
- Itemized invoice (nightly rate, cleaning fee, Airbnb service fee)
- Pre-approval email (AAR-06 form approval)

### Section 6.6: Group Accommodation
For group bookings (multiple employees sharing a property or block booking):
- List all occupants with employee IDs
- Indicate cost split per person
- One employee submits the full invoice; others submit "copy - reimbursed by [Employee ID]" annotation

Duplicate reimbursement of the same accommodation is flagged as fraud.
